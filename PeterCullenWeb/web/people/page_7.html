Mr Brendan Barry

Brendan Barry has been involved in water and the Murray Darling Basin for 35 years as the son of a Murray Valley irrigator; through secondary school and university student employment in irrigation enterprises in the Murrumbidgee Valley, and as farm agronomist for Tandou Limited. In his current role he manages the Tandouâ€™s significant water assets in the southern Murray Darling Basin. During the recent drought he joined the Lower Murray Water (Vic.) Customer Consultative Committee to advocate a market solution to ensure the survival of significant urban gardens throughout the Sunraysia area, as opposed to harsh restrictions on water use that were sacrificing 100 year old gardens.


In his 2 years as secretary of South Western Water Users (NSW) he had regular contact with local irrigator stakeholders and state and commonwealth policy makers in concluding the Murray Darling Basin Plan process. In his role with Tandou Limited he regularly advocates for, and provides input into, policy and decision-making in the southern Murray Darling Basin. Issues included water markets and regulation, pricing and improvements to customer service by regulating authorities. During the recent drought Brendan successfully coordinated a privately funded project to save water from evaporation and used that water to ensure permanent plantings survived. Brendan lives in Mildura with his wife and two children, and is a keen water skier and recreational river user.
